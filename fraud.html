Fraud

10 Bolgias. Seducers, flatterers, simonists, sorcerers, unscrupulous politicians, hypocrites, thieves, deceivers, schismatics and falsifiers.

1: Panderers and Seducers

Sin

Seducer: a person who entices someone into sexual activity.

Punishment

Forced to run back and forth away from whiping demons.

Poetic Justice

Seducers and panderers were like slave drivers, so now they must suffer the fate of a slave, constantly moved by threats and acts of violence.

People

Jason, Murdered men, abused women, and ruined the land.

2: Flatterers

Sin

Flatterer: a person who lavishes praise, often insincerely; a sycophant.

Punishment

Burried in Excrement.

Poetic Justice

The "bullsh*te" that comes from their mouths, that they now must stand in.

People

Thais, who returned her lover's question with false answers.

3: Guilty of Symony

Sin

Religious for profit.

Punishment

Burried head first with flames burning their feet.

People

Many clegry and popes, especially Pope Nicholas the Third.

4: Diviners, Astologers, and Magicians

Sin

Practice of determining the hidden significance or cause of events, sometimes foretelling the future, by various natural, psychological, and other techniques.

Punishment

They must walk forever with their heads facing backwards.

Poetic Justice

Wanted to look ahead and beyond in life, they are forced to forever look back.

People

Tiresias, Changed from a man to a woman.

5: Barrators

Sin

Those who betray a ship owner, or rightful owner of borrowed property.

Punishment

Forced under shipping tar by demons.

Poetic Justice

Burried in the tar of the ships they misused.

People

Fra gomita, would steal his masters gold and consort with his ememies.

6: Hypocrites

Sin

A person who indulges in hypocrisy

Punishment

Walk in circles with beautiful robes, but the inside is of lead.

Poetic Justice

Made to look beautiful, but the lead on the inside weighs on them just as their falsity secretly weighs on them.

People

Catalano and Loderingo, started a civil war based on thier meddling in florence's afairs.

7: Thieves

Sin

A person who steals another person's property, especially by stealth and without using force or violence.

Punishment

Constantly seaching for a home for their soul, because their bodies are constantly stolen and manipulated by snakes.

Poetic Justice

Stole in life, and had no respect for property, and are condemened to such a fate.

People

Vanni Fucci, for stealing a "scarcity" He later curses God.

8: Fradulent Councelors

Sin

Lying to some point and using cunning words to alter his mind and convince him to some point to think doing a certain thing is ok to do.

Punishment

Contained within flames shaped like tongues, because the advise they give is false and incorrect.

Poetic Justice

Stuck in tongues because the advide they gave was false.

People

Ulysses and Diomedes, both for fraud during the Trojan war. Guido for giving advice on how to defeat the pope's enemies, for which he was promised salvation (an impossibility).

9: Sowers of Scandal

Sin

Say and do things which cause distrust among one another, which results in arguments and fights.

Punishment

Forced to walk in a circle and silced when they reach the begining and re-heal as they walk around only to be cut again.

People

Mohammed.

10: Falsifiers

Sin

Leads to believe something that is not true.

Punishment

Of metals - plagued with scratchy scabs
Counterfeiters of other people - Turn on each other like animals
Of coins - mingle with the counterfeiters of people

People

Myrah, disguised herself as her mother to sleep with her father.
